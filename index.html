Day 27

How to set boundaries without feeling mean

This is for the girls who apologize when someone bumps into them. Who say yes when they mean no. Who feel guilty for taking up space.
You’ve been taught that boundaries are selfish. That good girls don’t say no. That keeping the peace means swallowing your discomfort.
I’m here to tell you: that’s a lie.
And it’s costing you more than you realize.My mother told me something abruptly one day. She said the things I was calling her out for would be the things I’d do myself in the future.
I was calling her out for always sacrificing herself in the name of family. For giving her “best” hoping for recognition from the elders, only to hear “this is what you’re supposed to do.”
She was teaching me that I’d do the same. “For the family’s sake.”
The moment those words left her mouth, I whispered to myself: No. I won’t live like this....

Day 74

The times I lose myself

I sometimes can’t recognise myself.
Not in the dramatic, cinematic way. Not the movie scene where someone looks in the mirror and doesn’t know the face looking back. Quieter than that. More ordinary. Just a moment, sometimes in the middle of a completely normal day, where I am suddenly a stranger inside my own brain. Where the hand at the end of my arm feels like it belongs to someone I haven’t been introduced to yet. Where the room I am sitting in, the same room I have sat in a hundred times, looks slightly off. Like a photograph of itself rather than the actual thing.
I don’t know what to call it. I am calling it losing myself, for now, because that is the closest I can get....
